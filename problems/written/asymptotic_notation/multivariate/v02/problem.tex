\begin{prob}
    Provide the asymptotic time complexity of the operation below using multivariate $\Theta$ notation.
    \\
    Given two d-dimensional vectors $v=[v_1,v_2,...,v_d]$ and $u=[u_1,u_2,...,u_d]$, their sum is computed as $v+u=[v_1+u_1, v_2+u_2,...,v_d+u_d]$. Given n vectors over $R^d$, check if any two of them sum to the zero vector using the brute force method.

    \begin{soln} 
        Computing the sum of two d dimensional vectors takes $\theta(d)$ time. In the brute force method, we compute the sum of every vector with every other vector.
        \\
        In the best case, we find two vectors which sum to the zero vector in the very first iteration. Therefore, the best case time complexity would be $\theta(d)$.
        \\
        In the worst case, we do not find a pair which sum to the zero vector. There are ${n \choose 2}$ pairs. We know that  $\binom{n}{2}=\theta(n^2)$. Therefore, computing the sum of $\binom{n}{2}$ pairs takes $\theta(n^2d)$ time.
        \\
        The average time complexity is $\theta(n^2d)$.
    \end{soln}

\end{prob}
