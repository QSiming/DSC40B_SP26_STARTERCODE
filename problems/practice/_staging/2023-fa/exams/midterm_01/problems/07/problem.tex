\begin{prob}
    What is the \textbf{expected} time complexity of the function below?
    State your answer using asymptotic notation.

    You may assume that \python{math.sqrt} and \python{math.log2} take
    $\Theta(1)$ time.

    \inputminted[lastline=15]{python}{\thisdir/code.py}

    \inlineresponsebox{$\Theta(n \log n)$}

    \begin{soln}
        There are three cases: Case 1) $x < \log_2 n$; Case 2) $1 < x < \sqrt n$; and
        Case 3) $x \geq \sqrt{n}$.

        The probability of Case 1 is $\log n / n$. The time taken in case 1 is
        $\Theta(n^2)$.

        The probability of Case 2 is \[
            \frac{\sqrt n}{n} - \frac{\log_2 n}{n} = \Theta(\sqrt n / n) = \Theta(1/\sqrt n).
        \] The time taken in this case is $\Theta(n)$.

        The probability of Case 3 is 1 minus the probability of the sum of the
        other two cases, which will simply be $\Theta(1)$: \[
            1 - \Theta(1 / n) - \Theta(1 / \sqrt{n}) = \Theta(1).
        \] The time taken is $\Theta(1)$.

        Therefore, the expected time is:
        \begin{align*}
            \frac{\log n}{n} \times \Theta(n^2)
            + \Theta(1/\sqrt n) \times \Theta(n)
            + \Theta(1) \times \Theta(1)
            &=
            \Theta(n \log n) + \Theta(\sqrt n) + \Theta(1)
            \\
            &=
            \Theta(n \log n)
        \end{align*}
    \end{soln}

\end{prob}
